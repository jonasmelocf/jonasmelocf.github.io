# Why coding feels magical

_special thanks to Grammarly_

Books are funny.

They're a collection of words written in a way that can entertain you, make you laugh, and even cry.
When reading Harry Potter, you will get yourself into a world inside your own head where everything feels adventurous and mystical.

For me, coding goes beyond just being both the writer and the reader. Coding is like playing Dungeons & Dragons, where you create an adventure by designing rules, challenges, and outcomes, shaping a world through your imagination—only here, that world comes alive in code.

Constructing a logic where a user sends product data that needs to be validated by the front end, then the back end, and then stored in the database feels like an adventure in my head.

It feels **magical** to take out the logic inside your head and type it out in english in the form of computer instructions using a keyboard just to see your machine do what you asked it to do.

As Gabe Newell once said,

"The programmers of tomorrow are the wizards of the future."
